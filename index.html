Ecosystem Overview

Mangrove forests are vital coastal ecosystems that protect shorelines, sustain marine biodiversity, improve water quality, and store massive amounts of carbon. Despite their importance, mangroves are increasingly threatened by pollution, habitat loss, and unsustainable human activities, making conservation and restoration efforts essential.
